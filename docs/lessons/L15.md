## Grammar Notes

### The 5 German Plural Types

| Type | Rule | Example | Notes |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| **+(e)** | Add -e | Tisch → Tische | Often M; often adds umlaut |
| **+(e)n** | Add -en or -n | Frau → Frauen; Lampe → Lampen | Often F |
| **+(e)r** | Add -er | Kind → Kinder; Buch → Bücher | Often N; often adds umlaut |
| **–** (no change) | Stays same | Fenster → Fenster; Zimmer → Zimmer | Often N ending in -er |
| **+s** | Add -s | Café → Cafés; Auto → Autos | Usually loanwords |

**GOLDEN RULE**: There is NO reliable plural prediction from the singular alone. **Always learn the plural with the noun!**
- Learn: **der Tisch, die Tische** (not just Tisch)
- Learn: **das Buch, die Bücher** (not just Buch)

**Plural article**: ALL plural nouns use **die** regardless of original gender!
- der Mann → die Männer ✅
- die Frau → die Frauen ✅  
- das Kind → die Kinder ✅

**die Leute** is a collective plural — never use it in singular (there is no "eine Leute"). Use **ein Mensch** for singular.

## Teacher Notes
- Slovak has plural too, but German's 5-type system with umlaut variants is genuinely harder — no shortcuts.
- The "always die for plural" rule is actually a relief — only ONE article for 4 genders in plural!
- die Leute and die Menschen both mean "people" but Leute is more colloquial/informal.
- Explicitly note: die Eltern, die Geschwister, die Leute — plurals with no singular form.
- This lesson bridges into L16 (modal verbs) nicely: Viele Leute können gut Deutsch sprechen.
