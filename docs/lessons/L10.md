# L10 — Akkusativ — Den/Einen

## Pedagogical Goal
Introduce the accusative case — the first case distinction that visibly changes article forms. This is a major grammar milestone. The key insight for learners: only masculine nouns change in the accusative (der→den, ein→einen, kein→keinen). Feminine and neuter forms stay identical to nominative. This is dramatically simpler than Slovak's full case system (7 cases, all genders change). The lesson is anchored in a café dialogue — Jana orders food and drink, which naturally requires haben, kaufen, nehmen + accusative object. This lesson introduces the FIRST DIALOGUE exercise in the app.

## Communicative Goal (what learner can do after)
Po tejto lekcii viem objednať jedlo a nápoj v kaviarni a správne použiť akuzatív mužského rodu (den/einen/keinen).

## Grammar Focus
**Accusative case** = direct object of the verb (wen? was? — koho? čo?)

**The accusative change table:**
| | Nominative (subject) | Accusative (direct object) |
|---|---|---|
| Maskulinum (der) | **der** Kaffee | **den** Kaffee |
| Maskulinum (ein) | **ein** Kaffee | **einen** Kaffee |
| Maskulinum (kein) | **kein** Kaffee | **keinen** Kaffee |
| Femininum (die) | **die** Suppe | **die** Suppe ← SAME |
| Femininum (eine) | **eine** Suppe | **eine** Suppe ← SAME |
| Neutrum (das) | **das** Wasser | **das** Wasser ← SAME |
| Neutrum (ein) | **ein** Wasser | **ein** Wasser ← SAME |

**RULE: Only masculine articles change in accusative. Feminine and neuter are identical to nominative.**

**Verbs that require accusative (transitive):**
- haben, kaufen, trinken, essen, sehen, brauchen, mögen, nehmen, bestellen

**Examples:**
- "Ich trinke **einen** Kaffee." (M → einen)
- "Ich esse **eine** Suppe." (F → eine, unchanged)
- "Ich habe **ein** Problem." (N → ein, unchanged)
- "Ich sehe **den** Mann." (M definite → den)

## Vocabulary Field
Food and drink (café), accusative verbs, ordering phrases.

## Slovak Contrastive Notes
- Slovak has 7 cases; German has 4. Slovak learners know EXACTLY what cases are — they have a HUGE advantage here.
- Slovak accusative (akuzatív) changes ALL genders: ten stôl→ten stôl (M inanimate stays the same), toho muža→toho muža... actually Slovak masculine animate DOES change: ten muž (nom) → toho muža (acc). German is SIMPLER — only masculine article changes, not the noun itself.
- Map Slovak to German: "V slovenčine sa v akuzatíve mení veľa vecí. V nemčine sa mení iba člen mužského rodu. To je oveľa jednoduchšie!"
- Slovak uses "-a" suffix on masculine animate nouns in accusative (vidím muž-a). German uses "den/einen" on the article but the noun form stays the same. This is a key difference — the case is marked on the ARTICLE in German, not the noun.
- "mögen" — Slovak "mať rád/rada" (to like) uses a completely different construction. German mögen is a modal-like verb conjugated as: ich mag, du magst, er mag — these forms are irregular and introduced as memorized forms here, with full modal treatment in L13.

## Exercise Sequence and Rationale
1. **Flashcard** — Nominative → Accusative transformations. Masculine nouns show the change; F/N show "same."
2. **MCQ** — 6 questions: choose correct accusative article; identify when change occurs (M only); one question on a verb that requires accusative.
3. **Fill** — Replace nominative with accusative in context: "Das ist ein Kaffee. → Ich trinke ___ Kaffee."
4. **Listen** — Hear an ordering sentence → identify the accusative object and its gender.
5. **Match** — Nominative form ↔ accusative form of the article.
6. **Dialogue** (FIRST IN THE APP!): Jana im Café — naturally uses all vocabulary from L07–L10; comprehension questions test accusative understanding.

## Jana's Narrative Context
Jana sedí v Café Central. Je jej prvý pracovný deň za sebou a ona si zaslúži odmenu. Pozná jedálny lístok (L08), vie čísla (L05-L06), vie sa sformovne spýtať (L02-L04). Teraz si musí objednať — a to znamená použiť akuzatív. "Ich nehme einen Kaffee und einen Kuchen." Čašník sa usmieva. Jana je spokojná — hovorí nemecky!

## Teacher Notes
- Frame the accusative announcement positively: "Toto je váš prvý pád v nemčine. A je OVEĽA jednoduchší ako v slovenčine."
- The "only masculine changes" rule is the golden rule to internalize. Use a mnemonic: "Der has something to hide — he puts on a disguise (den) when he's an object."
- The dialogue exercise is a milestone — learners have been building to this for 9 lessons. Make the dialogue natural and rewarding. Josef the waiter should be friendly, the exchange should feel achievable.
- "Ich habe Hunger / Ich habe Durst" — the haben idioms for hunger and thirst. These are fixed expressions (no article!) — they're important for café vocabulary but should be tagged as idiomatic.
- mögen ich mag / du magst / er mag — introduce as frozen forms for now. "Ich mag Kaffee" = I like coffee. Don't conjugate the full modal paradigm yet (L13).
- This lesson is the culmination of weeks 1–2. The dialogue should integrate ALL vocabulary from L01–L10. It's a mini-assessment in disguise.
