# L01 — Das Alphabet und Aussprache

## Pedagogical Goal
Lay the phonological foundation for all future German learning. Slovak learners must unlearn several sound-letter correspondences that differ between German and Slovak (V/W/Z especially) and learn Umlaut sounds that have no Slovak equivalent. Without solid pronunciation from day one, bad habits compound. This lesson uses a "hear-then-read" sequence: learners hear the letter name, connect it to a sound rule, then practice spelling aloud.

## Communicative Goal (what learner can do after)
Po tejto lekcii viem nahláskovat' svoje meno a iné slová po nemecky a rozumiem základným pravidlám čítania nemeckých písmen.

## Grammar Focus
No grammar per se — but the concept of "reading rules" is introduced: German spelling is largely phonemic (unlike English), which is reassuring for Slovak learners who also have a phonemic orthography.

## Vocabulary Field
Meta-linguistic: letter names, pronunciation descriptors, spelling conventions.

## Slovak Contrastive Notes
| German | Pronounced | Slovak equivalent | Trap |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| W | [v] | V in Slovak | Slovak learners often say [w] (English influence) |
| V | [f] | F in Slovak | Learners read it as Slovak V = [v] — WRONG in German |
| Z | [ts] | Slovak Z + S merged | No equivalent single letter |
| J | [j] | Y in Slovak words | Slovak learners may say [dʒ] (English influence) |
| ch (a/o/u) | [x] | CH in Slovak (chlieb) | Same sound — easy! |
| ch (e/i) | [ç] | No Slovak equivalent | Softer — like whispering "ch" |
| ß | [s] | SS in Slovak | No such letter in Slovak; replaced by SS after short vowel |
| ä | [ɛ] close to Slovak E | Almost like long Slovak E | Slovak ä exists but is rare and dialectal |
| ö | [ø] | No Slovak equivalent | Round lips as for O, say E |
| ü | [y] | No Slovak equivalent | Round lips as for U, say I |

## Exercise Sequence and Rationale
1. **Flashcard** — Letter → sound rule. Builds the mental mapping before testing begins. Learner sees/hears letter, flips to see pronunciation rule + example word.
2. **MCQ** — Pronunciation rules tested. "Wie spricht man 'W' aus?" Forces active retrieval of the most critical rules.
3. **Fill** — Complete the spelling sequence. "H-A-__-L-O = ?" Trains letter-name recall in spelling context.
4. **Listen** — Hear letter name → identify correct letter. Simulates real situations (spelling your name at a hotel check-in).
5. **Match** — Letter or letter combination → pronunciation rule. Consolidates the full set.

## Teacher Notes
- Slovak learners pick up ch=[x] instantly (identical to Slovak). Praise this — it builds confidence.
- The W/V swap is the biggest trap. Drill it with Wien (Viedeň) and Vogel immediately.
- Z=[ts] often surprises learners. Use "Pizza" as a bridge — they already know [ts] from Italian loanwords.
- ß: teach it as "ss after long vowel." Straße (long a) vs. Wasser (short a = ss). Don't over-complicate.
- Umlauts ä/ö/ü: describe articulatorily — don't just say "another vowel." Show lip position.
- Keep the "German is mostly phonemic — what you see is what you say" message front and center. It relieves anxiety.
- Reinforce throughout later lessons: every new word in L02+ should be demonstrated with correct pronunciation.
