# L01 — Begrüßung, Name und das Alphabet (Goethe A1 Lesson 1)

## Pedagogical Goal
Aligning with the standard Goethe-Institut A1 curriculum (Start Deutsch 1), the first module must enable immediate basic communication: exchanging greetings, asking/giving names, and spelling via the German alphabet. Slovak learners must unlearn different sound-letter correspondences (V/W/Z) while mastering "chunked" communicative phrases before deep grammar is introduced.

## Communicative Goal (what learner can do after)
Po tejto lekcii viem pozdraviť, predstaviť sa (svoje meno) a nahláskovať ho po nemecky. Rozumiem základnej nemeckej výslovnosti a abecede.

## Grammar Focus
- Formulaic chunks (Ich heiße..., Wie heißen Sie?, Mein Name ist...).
- Pronouns in chunks (Ich, Sie).
- Basic pronunciation phonemes (W, V, Z, ch, ß, Umlaute).

## Vocabulary Field
- Greetings and farewells (Guten Tag, Hallo, Tschüss, Auf Wiedersehen).
- Names and asking for names.
- The Alphabet and spelling.

## Slovak Contrastive Notes
| German | Pronounced | Slovak equivalent | Trap |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| W | [v] | V in Slovak | Slovak learners often say [w] |
| V | [f] | F in Slovak | Learners read it as Slovak V = [v] |
| Z | [ts] | Slovak C (cár) | No equivalent single letter |
| ß | [s] | SS in Slovak | No such letter in Slovak |

## Exercise Sequence and Rationale
1. **Flashcard** — Greetings, name phrases, and alphabet rules.
2. **MCQ** — Pronunciation checks (W/V/Z) and appropriate greeting selection based on time of day.
3. **Fill** — Complete chunks ("___ Tag", "Ich ___ Jana").
4. **Listen** — Hear letters and names being spelled.
5. **Writing** — Type "Guten Tag", "Ich heiße...", which correspond strictly to the taught vocab.
6. **Speaking** — Pronounce the chunks and spell names aloud.

## Jana's Narrative Context (Revised)
**Previous Context:** Jana at HR in Vienna (Too complex/unrealistic for absolute A1 day 1).
**New Context:** Jana registers at a German Language School in Vienna (Sprachschule) or at a Hotel reception. The receptionist says "Guten Tag", asks for her name, and asks her to spell the last name. 
*Why?* This limits the passive vocabulary to international cognates (Hotel, Rezeption, Name) and fits the communicative acts of Goethe A1 perfectly.

## Teacher / Curriculum Notes
- **Goethe Alignment:** Standard A1 lections never teach the alphabet in isolation without greetings. It must serve a communicative purpose (spelling your name to the receptionist).
- **Scope limit:** Keep grammar passive here. Do not conjugate "heißen" beyond ich/Sie/du. 
- **Correction strictly applied:** All writing exercises MUST only test what is listed in the vocabulary section.
