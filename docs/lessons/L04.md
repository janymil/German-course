# L04 — Wie heißt du? — W-Fragen und Wortstellung

## Pedagogical Goal
Introduce the sine qua non of German syntax: the Verb-Second (V2) rule. This lesson makes explicit what learners have been intuiting in L01–L03 — that the conjugated verb ALWAYS occupies the second position in a main clause. W-questions are the perfect vehicle for this: the W-word is in position 1, the verb is forced into position 2, and the subject follows. This is radically different from Slovak word order (Subject-Verb-Object default, with pragmatic flexibility).

## Communicative Goal (what learner can do after)
Po tejto lekcii viem položiť základné otázky (Ako sa voláš? Odkiaľ si? Koľko máš rokov?) a odpovedať na ne — formálne aj neformálne.

## Grammar Focus
**Verb-Second (V2) rule** in declarative sentences:
- Element 1 (any) + **VERB** + Subject (if not in pos.1) + rest
- "Woher **kommen** Sie?" — Woher = pos.1, kommen = pos.2
- "Ich **komme** aus Bratislava." — Ich = pos.1, komme = pos.2
- "Aus Bratislava **komme** ich." — inverted, verb still pos.2

**W-question words** (no inversion of subject/verb — verb already in pos.2):
| German | Slovak |
|--------|--------|
| wer | kto |
| was | čo |
| wo | kde |
| woher | odkiaľ |
| wohin | kam |
| wie | ako |
| wann | kedy |
| warum | prečo |
| wie alt | koľko rokov |

**Formal vs informal question forms:**
- Informal: "Wie heißt **du**?"
- Formal: "Wie heißen **Sie**?"

## Vocabulary Field
Question words, discourse-level vocabulary for introductions, kommen (to come from).

## Slovak Contrastive Notes
- Slovak has NO fixed V2 rule. "Kde bývaš?", "Bývaš kde?" and "Kde ty bývaš?" are all grammatical Slovak — emphasis shifts but sentence is valid. In German, the verb must be in position 2. Period.
- Slovak question words map neatly to German: kde→wo, kto→wer, čo→was, ako→wie, kedy→wann, prečo→warum, odkiaľ→woher, kam→wohin.
- "Kommen" = "prísť / pochádzať." In German, "Ich komme aus..." always means origin; Slovak uses "pochádzam z..." or "som z..." — both are valid in Slovak.
- Slovak learners will naturally try to put the verb at the end: "*Woher du kommst?*" — this is a calque from Slovak and must be corrected explicitly.
- The formal question requires inversion of Sie and verb: "Wie heißen Sie?" — in Slovak formal questions, the verb position doesn't change, so this feels odd.

## Exercise Sequence and Rationale
1. **Flashcard** — W-question words: German → Slovak. Learner must know all 7 cold.
2. **MCQ** — V2 rule: "Which sentence is correct?" Multiple choice between correct and misordered sentences. Also tests formal vs informal.
3. **Fill** — Complete the W-question or the answer with the correct form of kommen/wohnen.
4. **Listen** — Hear a Q&A exchange → identify whether it is formal or informal.
5. **Match** — W-question word ↔ Slovak equivalent.

## Jana's Narrative Context
Jana prichádza na pohovor do firmy v prvom okrese Viedne. Dr. Müller, jej budúci šéf, ju víta formálne: "Guten Tag, wie heißen Sie?" Jana odpovedá: "Guten Tag, mein Name ist Jana Nováková." Dr. Müller sa pýta: "Woher kommen Sie?" Jana odpovedá: "Ich komme aus der Slowakei, aus Bratislava." Rozhovor pokračuje formálne — Jana si uvedomuje, že musí používať Sie, nie du.

## Teacher Notes
- V2 is arguably the most important syntactic rule in German. Introduce it as a rule, not just a pattern: "The conjugated verb ALWAYS sits in seat 2 in a main clause. Always. Seat 1 can be anything."
- Use a visual "traffic light" or "seat diagram" — chairs numbered 1, 2, 3. Verb always sits in chair 2. This spatial metaphor works very well with adult learners.
- The W-question doesn't "invert" — it's just that the W-word takes position 1 and the verb is STILL in position 2. Students sometimes think there's a special rule for questions; there isn't — it's always V2.
- The dialogue in this lesson (Jana meeting Dr. Müller) is the first extended dialogue — but it uses only vocabulary from L01–L04. This reassures learners that they can already DO something real.
- Note: "kommen aus" takes bare country names (Ich komme aus Österreich) EXCEPT for countries with definite article (Ich komme aus der Slowakei). This exception is flagged here and fully treated in L09.
