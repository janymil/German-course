## Grammar Notes

### müssen conjugation
| ich | du | er/sie/es | wir | ihr | sie/Sie |
|-----|----|-----------|-----|-----|---------|
| **muss** | **musst** | **muss** | **müssen** | **müsst** | **müssen** |

Note: ich = er/sie/es (muss), same as können!

### dürfen conjugation
| ich | du | er/sie/es | wir | ihr | sie/Sie |
|-----|----|-----------|-----|-----|---------|
| **darf** | **darfst** | **darf** | **dürfen** | **dürft** | **dürfen** |

### ⚠️ THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION

**dürfen nicht** = **must not** (prohibition — FORBIDDEN)
**nicht müssen** = **don't have to** (not obligatory — but OK if you want)

| German | Slovak | Meaning |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Du **darfst** nicht rauchen. | Nesmieš fajčiť. | It is **forbidden** |
| Du **musst** nicht kommen. | Nemusíš prísť. | You don't **have to** come (but you can) |

This is one of the most commonly confused areas in German for ALL language learners — not just Slovak speakers!

### Satzklammer — reinforced
- Ich **muss** heute viel **arbeiten**. (I have to work a lot today)
- Jana **darf** hier nicht **rauchen**. (Jana is not allowed to smoke here)
- Du **musst** leise **sein**. (You have to be quiet)

### man — the impersonal pronoun
- **Man** darf hier nicht parken. = One is not allowed to park here. / You can't park here.
- **Man** muss leise sein. = One must be quiet.
- man = Slovak "sa" construction or "človek" — always third person singular!

## Teacher Notes
- dürfen nicht vs. nicht müssen causes serious mistakes in real life (huge difference between "forbidden" and "not obligatory"!).
- man as impersonal subject is very useful — covers public sign language and general statements.
- Austrian context: signs in Vienna use both German formal and informal Register. Jana will encounter Rauchen verboten, Kein Eingang, etc.
- Link back to kein (L11): Kein Rauchen! = Rauchen verboten! Both are common on signs.
